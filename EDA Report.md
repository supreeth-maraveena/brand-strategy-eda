# Brand Strategy Analysis Report

## 1. Objective

This analysis evaluates the relationship between **Average Price**, **Stockout Rate**, and **Lost Revenue** across brands. The goal is to identify demand patterns, inventory inefficiencies, and strategic opportunities for optimization.

---

## 2. Key Observations

### 2.1 Price vs Stockout Trend

A weak negative relationship is observed:

* As **price increases**, **stockout rate tends to decrease**
* Lower-priced products face **higher stockout frequency**

**Interpretation:**
Demand is concentrated in **low to mid-priced segments**, while premium products experience relatively stable availability.

---

### 2.2 Demand Concentration

A dense cluster exists in:

* **Price range:** 20–40
* **Stockout rate:** 0.3–0.6

**Implication:**

* These products are **high-demand items**
* Current inventory levels are insufficient

---

## 3. Quadrant Analysis

### 3.1 High Stockout – Low Price (Top-Left Quadrant)

* Represents the **largest concentration of brands**
* Indicates **strong demand but poor inventory planning**

**Strategy:**

* Increase stock levels
* Improve demand forecasting

---

### 3.2 High Stockout – High Price (Top-Right Quadrant)

Examples: Topshop, Pull&Bear, Mango

**Insights:**

* Premium products with **unexpectedly high demand**
* Indicates strong brand appeal or trend-driven sales

**Strategy:**

* Increase inventory
* Consider **price optimization (increase margins)**

---

### 3.3 Low Stockout – Low Price (Bottom-Left Quadrant)

* Balanced supply-demand region

**Strategy:**

* Maintain current inventory levels
* Monitor for demand shifts

---

### 3.4 Low Stockout – High Price (Bottom-Right Quadrant)

* Many brands fall into this category

**Insights:**

* Products are likely **overstocked or slow-moving**

**Strategy:**

* Introduce discounts or promotions
* Reduce future inventory orders

---

## 4. Lost Revenue Analysis

* Larger bubble sizes indicate **higher lost revenue due to stockouts**
* Significant revenue loss is observed in:

  * **Mid-price range (40–60)**
  * Moderate-to-high stockout regions

**Implication:**

* The company is losing substantial revenue in its **core pricing segment**

---

## 5. Outlier Insights

* Certain brands (e.g., mid-to-high priced with high stockout rates) show:

  * Strong demand
  * Poor inventory allocation

**Action:**

* Immediate restocking required
* Prioritize these brands in supply chain planning

---

## 6. Strategic Recommendations

### 6.1 Inventory Optimization

* Increase stock for **high-demand, low-price products**
* Reallocate inventory from low-demand segments

---

### 6.2 Pricing Strategy

* Increase prices for:

  * High-demand premium products
* Introduce discounts for:

  * Overstocked high-price items

---

### 6.3 Demand Forecasting

* Implement data-driven forecasting models
* Focus on mid-price segment where demand variability is highest

---

### 6.4 Revenue Recovery

* Prioritize reducing stockouts in:

  * Mid-price, high-demand products
* This segment offers the **highest potential revenue gain**

---

## 7. Conclusion

The analysis reveals:

* A mismatch between **inventory allocation and demand distribution**
* Strong demand concentrated in **low to mid-priced products**
* Significant revenue loss due to **stockouts in key segments**

By optimizing inventory distribution, adjusting pricing strategies, and improving demand forecasting, the business can:

* Reduce lost revenue
* Improve customer satisfaction
* Enhance overall operational efficiency
